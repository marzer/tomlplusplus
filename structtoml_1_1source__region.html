Remark

toml++'s parser is unicode-aware insofar as it knows how to handle various non-conventional whitespace and newline characters, but it doesn't give much thought to combining marks, grapheme clusters vs. characters, et cetera. If a TOML document contains lots of codepoints outside of the ASCII range you may find that your source_positions don't match those given by a text editor (typically the line numbers will be accurate but column numbers will be too high). This is not an error. I've chosen this behaviour as a deliberate trade-off between parser complexity and correctness.
